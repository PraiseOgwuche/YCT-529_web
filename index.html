Literature Review

Molecular mechanisms, signaling pathways, and contraceptive efficacy of YCT-529

Retinoic Acid Signaling in Spermatogenesis

  • Vitamin A derivative essential for gene regulation via RAR-RXR heterodimers
  • Activates Stra8 and Kit genes essential for spermatogonial differentiation
  • Gradient patterns regulate spatiotemporal progression of spermatogenic cycle
  • Pulsatile release (every 8.6 days) drives synchronized differentiation
  • Critical for meiotic initiation through chromatin remodeling and repression of NANOS2
Key Enzymes: ALDH1A1, ALDH1A2, RDH10

Molecular Structure & Antagonist Mechanism

  • IUPAC: 4-[5-[2,2-dimethyl-4-(4-methylphenyl)chromen-6-yl]-1H-pyrrol-2-yl]benzoic acid
  • Molecular weight: 435.5 g/mol, LogP: 5.706
  • Rigid fused-ring system enhances RARα selectivity (Ki = 1.5 nM vs. 898 nM for RARβ)
  • Stabilizes RARα in inactive conformation, preventing coactivator recruitment
  • Promotes NCOR1/NCOR2 association and maintains histone deacetylation
Binding Affinity: Ki = 1.5 nM (RARα)

Pharmacokinetics & ADME Properties

  • Half-life: 11 hours with sustained testicular accumulation
  • High plasma protein binding (~98.8%) with moderate oral bioavailability
  • Primary metabolism via CYP3A4, CYP2C8, and CYP2C9
  • Phase II conjugation through UGT1A1/UGT2B7 (glucuronidation) and SULT1E1 (sulfation)
  • Cmax: 2.1 μM with delayed biliary excretion
Dosing Range: 10-20 mg/kg/day

Preclinical Efficacy & Safety

  • 99% reduction in sperm production in murine models
  • Complete infertility achieved within 4 weeks of treatment
  • Full fertility recovery 4-6 weeks post-withdrawal
  • No effect on testosterone levels or sexual function
  • 100% suppression of sperm production in non-human primates
Adverse Effects: No systemic toxicity detected

Retinoic Acid Signaling Pathway

Key Molecular Interactions:

  1. Retinol (ROL) is oxidized to retinal by RDH10 in Sertoli cells
  2. Retinal is converted to retinoic acid (RA) by ALDH1A1 and ALDH1A2
  3. RA binds to RAR-RXR heterodimers at retinoic acid response elements (RAREs)
  4. This complex recruits coactivators (NCOA1, NCOA2) and displaces corepressors (NCOR1, NCOR2)
  5. Histone acetylation by HATs promotes chromatin relaxation and gene activation
  6. Transcription of Stra8, Kit, and other genes initiates meiosis and germ cell differentiation

Gene Expression Dynamics:

  • Stra8: Critical for meiotic initiation, activated directly by RA
  • Kit: Promotes spermatogonial proliferation and differentiation
  • NANOS2: Inhibits meiosis entry, downregulated by RA signaling
  • Rec8: Essential for chromosome synapsis during meiosis
  • Dmc1: Required for homologous recombination

YCT-529 blocks this signaling cascade by binding to RARα, preventing RA binding and maintaining corepressor association, thus inhibiting the transcription of genes essential for spermatogenesis.

Molecular Structure and Binding Mechanism

YCT-529 Chemical Structure

4-[5-[2,2-dimethyl-4-(4-methylphenyl)chromen-6-yl]-1H-pyrrol-2-yl]benzoic acid

Structural Features Enhancing Selectivity:

  • Chromene Core: Provides molecular rigidity and hydrophobic interactions
  • Pyrrole Linker: Enables π-π stacking with aromatic residues in the binding pocket
  • Benzoic Acid Moiety: Forms critical hydrogen bonds with Ser232 in RARα
  • 2,2-Dimethyl Groups: Enhance metabolic stability and prevent oxidation
  • 4-Methylphenyl Substituent: Occupies hydrophobic pocket, improving binding affinity

Structural Basis for RARα Selectivity:

YCT-529's high selectivity for RARα (Ki = 1.5 nM) over RARβ (Ki = 898 nM) and RARγ (Ki = 10,618 nM) is primarily due to the unique Ser232 residue in RARα's ligand-binding domain. This residue, replaced by Ala225 in RARβ and Ala234 in RARγ, creates a polar interaction site that stabilizes YCT-529's binding. The benzoic acid moiety forms specific hydrogen bonds with this residue, while the rigid fused-ring system ensures optimal positioning for these interactions.

ADME Properties:

  • Absorption: Moderate oral bioavailability (42%) with peak plasma levels at 1-3 hours post-dose
  • Distribution: High plasma protein binding (98.8%) with significant testicular accumulation
  • Metabolism: Primarily by CYP3A4 (hydroxylation), with additional contributions from CYP2C8 and CYP2C9
  • Excretion: Predominantly through biliary elimination after Phase II conjugation (glucuronidation via UGT1A1/UGT2B7)

Predicted Population Differences:

PBPK modeling suggests potential variations in YCT-529 pharmacokinetics across ethnic groups, with East Asians showing higher systemic exposure due to reduced CYP3A4 activity, while Black Americans exhibit more rapid clearance. These differences may necessitate population-specific dosing regimens for optimal contraceptive efficacy.

Population-specific modeling and simulation of YCT-529 pharmacokinetics

Methodology Overview

This study employed physiologically based pharmacokinetic (PBPK) modeling to characterize YCT-529's behavior across diverse populations and dosing regimens.

Computational Tools: PK-Sim, ADMETLab 3.0, R Studio

Key Findings

Rapid Absorption

YCT-529 showed rapid absorption with Tmax occurring within 1–3 hours and dose-dependent increases in systemic exposure.

Population Differences

East Asians showed the highest systemic retention (Cmax ~500 μg/L at 180 mg), while Black Americans had the lowest (~300 μg/L), consistent with CYP3A4 metabolic variability.

Dietary Impact

Fasting increased Cmax to ~100 μg/L, while high-fat meals reduced it to ~55 μg/L, suggesting food affects absorption kinetics.

Model Validation

Atazanavir pharmacokinetics closely matched clinical data, supporting the validity of the simulation approach.

Statistical Significance: p < 0.001 for population differences

Dietary Effects on Pharmacokinetics

Key Observations

  • Fasting Condition: Highest peak concentration (Cmax ~100 μg/L) and more rapid absorption
  • High-Fat Meal: 45% reduction in Cmax (~55 μg/L) with delayed Tmax
  • Light Meal: Intermediate effect with ~25% reduction in peak concentration
  • AUC Differences: Total exposure (AUC) varied less dramatically than Cmax across conditions

Clinical Implications

Food significantly affects the rate and extent of YCT-529 absorption. For consistent drug exposure, administration timing relative to meals should be standardized. Morning administration in a fasting state may provide optimal and consistent drug levels.

Detailed Methodology Workflow

Computational Workflow Steps:

  1. SMILES String Generation: Encoded YCT-529's molecular structure in SMILES format for computational analysis
  2. Physicochemical Prediction: Used ADMETLab 3.0 to predict absorption, distribution, metabolism, excretion properties
  3. PK-Sim Modeling: Created physiologically-based pharmacokinetic models incorporating predicted parameters
  4. Population Simulation: Generated synthetic populations representing different ethnicities and dietary conditions
  5. Data Processing: Analyzed simulation outputs across 16,000 virtual participants
  6. Validation: Compared predicted values with known pharmacokinetic profiles of reference compounds
  7. Clinical Recommendations: Developed potential dosing strategies based on population differences

This computational approach allowed us to predict how YCT-529 would behave across different populations and under various dietary conditions without requiring extensive clinical trials, providing a foundation for future human studies.

Retinoic Acid Signaling in Spermatogenesis

Retinoic acid (RA) plays a crucial role in regulating spermatogenesis. As a metabolite of vitamin A, it activates nuclear receptors that control gene expression patterns essential for germ cell development, differentiation, and maturation.

RA signaling coordinates the differentiation of spermatogonia, initiates meiosis, and ensures proper sperm maturation throughout the spermatogenic cycle.
